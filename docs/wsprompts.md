## THE 4 TIME TIERS

### Overview

| Tier | Time Available | What They Get | Philosophy |
|------|---------------|---------------|------------|
| Tier 1: Minimum Viable | Under 30 min/week | 1 action per week | "Something is better than nothing" |
| Tier 2: Foundation | 30-60 min/week | 2 actions per week | "Build the base" |
| Tier 3: Full System | 1-2 hrs/week | 4 actions per week | "The complete loop" |
| Tier 4: Growth Mode | 2+ hrs/week | Full system + extras | "Optimize and expand" |

**Design principle:** Build the system 20% smaller than their stated capacity. Leave breathing room. People overestimate their available time.

## TIER 1: MINIMUM VIABLE (Under 30 min/week)

**For clients who said:** "Under 30 minutes" or "None right now"
**Philosophy:** One consistent action beats zero ambitious ones. This tier is about building the habit before building the system.

### The System

| When | Action | Time | What to Do |
|------|--------|------|-----------|
| Pick ONE day | 1 Visibility Action | 15-20 min | Choose ONE of the following each week |

**Visibility Action Options (pick the one that fits best):**

1. **Post one piece of content** — Use CE-1 and CE-2 prompts to brainstorm and draft. Post on your most active platform. (15 min)
2. **Send 3 client check-ins** — Use RL-1 prompt. Text or message 3 past clients. (10 min)
3. **Ask for 1 review** — Use RL-2 prompt. Send to one happy client. (5 min)
4. **Respond to engagement** — Check your DMs and comments. Respond to anyone who reached out. Use CM-1 if needed. (10 min)
5. **Share one Story** — Take a photo at work, add a sentence, post as a Story. No prompt needed. (5 min)

**What "done" looks like each week:** You did ONE thing. That's it. That's a win.

**For clients who said "None right now":**
> Start even smaller. Your only action this week: text 1 past client using RL-1. That's 5 minutes. That's your entire marketing system for Week 1. Next week, do 2. Build from there.

## TIER 2: FOUNDATION (30-60 min/week)

**For clients who said:** "30-60 minutes"
**Philosophy:** Two consistent actions create a foundation. One gets you seen. One keeps people coming back.

### The System

| When | Action | Time | What to Do |
|------|--------|------|-----------|
| Day 1 (e.g., Tuesday) | Visibility | 20-25 min | Create and post 1 piece of content (CE-1 + CE-2) |
| Day 2 (e.g., Friday) | Retention | 15-20 min | Send 3-5 client check-ins or ask for 1 review (RL-1 or RL-2) |

**Total:** 35-45 minutes/week

**What "done" looks like each week:**
- 1 post published (or scheduled)
- 3-5 past clients contacted

**Suggested rhythm:**
- After your last client on [Day 1], spend 20 minutes on content
- Before you leave on [Day 2], spend 15 minutes on client outreach

**Tip:** Attach these actions to an existing routine. "After my last client on Tuesday" is easier to remember than "sometime this week."

## TIER 3: FULL SYSTEM (1-2 hrs/week)

**For clients who said:** "1-2 hours"
**Philosophy:** This is the complete client-attraction loop — visibility, trust, retention, and referrals. Everything working together.

### The System

| When | Action | Time | What to Do |
|------|--------|------|-----------|
| Day 1 (e.g., Monday) | Visibility | 30-40 min | Batch 2-3 posts for the week (CE-1 + CE-2 + CE-4) |
| Day 2 (e.g., Wednesday) | Trust | 15-20 min | Post 1 social proof or educational piece (use prompts.md Ideas 1, 2, or 3) |
| Day 3 (e.g., Friday) | Retention | 15-20 min | Send 3-5 client check-ins (RL-1) |
| Day 4 (e.g., Saturday) | Referral | 10-15 min | Ask 1-2 clients for reviews or referrals (RL-2 or RL-3) |

**Total:** 70-95 minutes/week

**What "done" looks like each week:**
- 2-3 posts published
- 1 trust/authority piece published
- 3-5 past clients contacted
- 1-2 review or referral asks sent

**Monthly add-on (first week of each month):**
- Run the Monthly Content Calendar prompt (BP-3 from ncworkflowprompts.md) to plan the month ahead
- Send 3-5 referral asks (RL-3) — monthly, not weekly, to avoid fatigue

## TIER 4: GROWTH MODE (2+ hrs/week)

**For clients who said:** "2+ hours"
**Philosophy:** Everything from Tier 3, plus batch content creation and light analytics. This is optimization mode.

### The System

| When | Action | Time | What to Do |
|------|--------|------|-----------|
| Day 1 (e.g., Monday) | Content Batch | 45-60 min | Run full Content Engine workflow (ncworkflow.md Workflow 1) — produce 5-7 posts for the week/biweek |
| Day 2 (e.g., Wednesday) | Trust + Engagement | 20-25 min | Post 1 trust piece + respond to all comments/DMs from the week (CM-1) |
| Day 3 (e.g., Friday) | Retention | 15-20 min | 3-5 client check-ins (RL-1) + 1-2 post-appointment follow-ups (RL-4) |
| Day 4 (e.g., Saturday) | Referral + Review | 15-20 min | Monthly: referral asks (RL-3) + review requests (RL-2). Weekly: respond to engagement |
| End of week | Weekly Review | 10-15 min | Use the Weekly Review Prompt (below) to assess what worked |

**Total:** 105-140 minutes/week

**What "done" looks like each week:**
- 5-7 posts batched and scheduled
- 1 trust/authority piece published
- All DMs and comments responded to
- 3-5 client check-ins sent
- 1-2 referral or review asks sent
- Quick review of what's working

**Monthly add-ons:**
- Monthly Content Calendar (BP-3)
- Monthly referral push (RL-3 to 3-5 top clients)
- Review numbers: "How many new clients this month? Where did they come from?"


## ONGOING PLANNING & REVIEW PROMPTS

These prompts are given to the client so they can maintain and adjust their system independently.

---

### WS-1: Monday Planning Prompt

**When to use:** Start of each week. Takes 5 minutes. Sets your intention for the week.

```
I'm a [YOUR SERVICE TYPE]. Here's my schedule this week:
[LIST YOUR WORK DAYS AND APPROXIMATE SCHEDULE]

My marketing system has [NUMBER] actions per week:
[LIST YOUR WEEKLY ACTIONS — e.g., "1 content post, 3 client check-ins, 1 review ask"]

Help me plan when to do each action this week. Consider:
- I'm busiest on [BUSIEST DAYS]
- My lightest day is [LIGHTEST DAY]
- I have about [TIME] available for marketing this week

Give me a simple schedule: which action on which day, and when. Keep it realistic.
```

---

### WS-2: Monthly Content Calendar Prompt

**When to use:** First week of each month. Takes 10-15 minutes. Plans your content for the next 4 weeks.

```
I'm a [YOUR SERVICE TYPE] in [YOUR CITY/AREA]. I post on [YOUR PLATFORMS] about [FREQUENCY].

It's [MONTH/YEAR]. Create my content calendar for this month.

Include:
- [NUMBER] posts spread across the month
- A mix of content types: educational, trust-building, social proof, behind-the-scenes, and engagement
- Any seasonal or timely angles for [MONTH] (holidays, seasonal trends in my industry, etc.)
- One promotional post (max)

For each post give me:
- Suggested date
- Content type
- Post idea (1-2 sentences)
- Which prompt to use to draft it (CE-1, CE-2, etc.)

Keep ideas specific to my industry. Make the calendar feel manageable, not overwhelming.
```

---

### WS-3: Weekly Review Prompt

**When to use:** End of each week. Takes 5-10 minutes. Helps you see what's working.

```
Here's what I did for marketing this week:

Posts published: [NUMBER — and briefly what they were about]
Client check-ins sent: [NUMBER]
Reviews requested: [NUMBER]
Referral asks: [NUMBER]
New bookings this week: [NUMBER]
Where they came from: [REFERRAL / SOCIAL MEDIA / REPEAT / WALK-IN / OTHER]

Any engagement highlights: [e.g., "Got 3 comments on my educational post" or "A past client rebooked after my check-in text"]

Based on this, tell me:
1. What worked well (keep doing)
2. What I could adjust next week
3. One thing to try that I haven't yet

Keep your response under 200 words. Be encouraging but honest.
```

---

### WS-4: Capacity Adjustment Prompt

**When to use:** When your schedule or capacity changes and you need to adapt your system.

```
My marketing system was designed for [ORIGINAL TIME TIER — e.g., "1-2 hours per week"].

But my situation has changed: [EXPLAIN WHAT CHANGED — e.g., "I'm fully booked and have less time" or "I have more free time now" or "I'm feeling burned out" or "I want to ramp up because business is slow"].

My new available time is approximately [NEW TIME AVAILABLE] per week.

Adjust my weekly system to fit my new capacity. Keep the highest-impact actions and drop the lowest-impact ones first.

Show me:
- My adjusted weekly schedule
- What I'm keeping
- What I'm dropping (and why it's okay to drop it)
- When I should reassess again
```

## SYSTEM DESIGN PRINCIPLES (FOR KARLI'S REFERENCE)

1. **Start smaller than feels necessary.** If they say 1-2 hours, build for 70 minutes. Underpromise, overdeliver.

2. **Attach to existing routines.** "After your last client" > "every Wednesday." Habit stacking works.

3. **Make Week 1 embarrassingly easy.** If the full system has 4 actions, Week 1 should have 1-2. Build momentum, not overwhelm.

4. **Define "done."** Every week should have a clear finish line. "Did I do my actions? Yes? Done. Stop." No guilt about not doing more.

5. **Build in natural checkpoints.** Monthly review, not daily anxiety. "How did this month go?" beats "Am I doing enough today?"

6. **No guilt architecture.** If they miss a week, the system doesn't punish them. They just pick up where they left off. Design for humans, not robots.

7. **Tier migration is natural.** Most clients start at Tier 1-2 and naturally move up as marketing becomes a habit. Don't push them to a higher tier before they're ready.

8. **The system serves the business, not the other way around.** If the system becomes a source of stress, simplify it. The goal is clients and income, not checking boxes.
