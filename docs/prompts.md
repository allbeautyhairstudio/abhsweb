### IDEA 1: The Educational Tip

**Content Type:** Educational
**Purpose:** Positions you as an expert. Gives people a reason to follow you. Attracts clients who value knowledge.

**Template:**
> Did you know? [COMMON MISCONCEPTION OR LITTLE-KNOWN FACT ABOUT YOUR SERVICE].
>
> Here's what I tell my clients: [YOUR EXPERT ADVICE — 2-3 sentences].
>
> [OPTIONAL: A simple tip they can try at home]
>
> If you want help with [RELATED SERVICE], I'd love to work with you. Link in bio / [BOOKING LINK].

**AI Generation Prompt:**
```
I'm a [SERVICE TYPE] in [CITY/AREA]. Write an educational social media post about [TOPIC — e.g., "why you shouldn't wash your hair every day" or "how to maintain results between appointments"].

Include:
- A surprising fact or tip that most people don't know
- A brief explanation in plain language (2-3 sentences)
- A gentle call to action inviting them to book

Keep it under 120 words. Warm and knowledgeable tone — like explaining something to a friend, not lecturing.
```

**Platform Notes:** Works on all platforms. Especially strong on Instagram (carousel or caption), Facebook, and Google Business posts.
**Time to Create:** 5-10 minutes
**Archetype Notes:**
- Overwhelmed Poster: Great for batching — generate 4-5 educational tips in one sitting
- Avoider: Can be shared as a text/message to existing clients instead of posting publicly

### IDEA 2: The Trust Builder

**Content Type:** Trust-Building
**Purpose:** Shows clients why they should choose you over someone else. Builds credibility without bragging.

**Template:**
> One thing I always [DO/USE/PRIORITIZE] with every client is [YOUR STANDARD OR PRACTICE].
>
> Why? Because [EXPLANATION OF WHY THIS MATTERS TO THE CLIENT — not to you].
>
> It's a small thing, but it makes a big difference in [OUTCOME CLIENT CARES ABOUT].
>
> That's the kind of experience I want every client to have.

**AI Generation Prompt:**
```
I'm a [SERVICE TYPE]. Write a trust-building social media post about [SPECIFIC PRACTICE — e.g., "why I use specific product brands" or "my consultation process" or "how I customize every service"].

Focus on why this matters to the CLIENT, not just that I do it. Make me sound thoughtful and intentional — not braggy.

Keep it under 100 words. End with something that makes people feel safe choosing me.
```

**Platform Notes:** Strong on Instagram (with a photo of you working), Facebook, Google Business.
**Time to Create:** 5-10 minutes
**Archetype Notes:**
- Overwhelmed Poster: Use this to replace random posts with intentional ones
- Avoider: Share the trust message verbally with clients during appointments — it doesn't have to be a post

### IDEA 3: The Social Proof Spotlight

**Content Type:** Social Proof
**Purpose:** Nothing sells like other people's positive experiences. Reviews and transformations build trust faster than anything you can say about yourself.

**Template:**
> Client love [heart emoji or equivalent]
>
> "[ACTUAL CLIENT QUOTE OR PARAPHRASED FEEDBACK]"
>
> [If applicable: BEFORE/AFTER DESCRIPTION or "Swipe to see the transformation"]
>
> This is why I do what I do. Moments like these.
>
> Ready for your own [SERVICE RESULT]? [BOOKING LINK]

**AI Generation Prompt:**
```
I'm a [SERVICE TYPE]. A client recently said: "[CLIENT QUOTE OR PARAPHRASE OF THEIR POSITIVE FEEDBACK]." The service they got was [SERVICE].

Write a social proof post that:
- Highlights their experience naturally (not over-the-top)
- Connects their result to what other potential clients might want
- Ends with an invitation to book

Keep it under 80 words. Genuine and warm — not like a testimonial ad.
```

**Platform Notes:** Instagram (with before/after photo if available), Facebook, Google Business. Before/after photos are the highest-engaging content for most service providers.
**Time to Create:** 5 minutes (if you have the photo/quote ready)
**Archetype Notes:**
- Overwhelmed Poster: Post these on days when you don't know what else to say — they're easy and effective
- Avoider: Even if you don't post, screenshot reviews and keep them in a folder. Share them when someone asks "Is this worth it?"

### IDEA 4: The Behind-the-Scenes

**Content Type:** Behind-the-Scenes
**Purpose:** Makes you relatable and human. People book with people they feel connected to. This lets them see the real you.

**Template:**
> A peek behind the scenes [OR: What my [DAY/MORNING/WORKSPACE] actually looks like]
>
> [DESCRIBE SOMETHING REAL — setting up your space, prepping products, your morning routine before clients arrive, a funny moment during the day]
>
> [PERSONAL REFLECTION — why you love this, what surprised you, what you're grateful for]
>
> This is the real work behind the results.

**AI Generation Prompt:**
```
I'm a [SERVICE TYPE]. Write a behind-the-scenes social media post about [SCENARIO — e.g., "my morning routine before clients arrive" or "how I set up my workspace" or "what happens between appointments"].

Make it feel authentic and personal — like they're getting a peek into my real day. Include a moment of personality (humor, warmth, or honesty).

Keep it under 100 words. No corporate tone. This should feel like a story, not an ad.
```

**Platform Notes:** Instagram Stories (quick, casual), Instagram feed (with a real photo), Facebook, TikTok.
**Time to Create:** 5 minutes — snap a photo, write a caption (or use the AI prompt)
**Archetype Notes:**
- Overwhelmed Poster: Use Stories for behind-the-scenes — they disappear in 24 hours, which lowers the pressure
- Avoider: Take the photo even if you don't post it. Build a library. When you're ready, you'll have content waiting

### IDEA 5: The Community Connection

**Content Type:** Community Positioning
**Purpose:** Positions you as part of your local community, not just a business. People support people they feel connected to. Cross-promotes local businesses (they may share your post, doubling your reach).

**Template:**
> One of my favorite [LOCAL BUSINESSES / SPOTS / PEOPLE] in [YOUR AREA] is [BUSINESS NAME].
>
> [WHY YOU LOVE THEM — 1-2 sentences about what they do or why you recommend them]
>
> If you're in the area, check them out. Supporting local matters.
>
> Who's YOUR favorite local spot? Drop it below [pointing down emoji or equivalent]

**AI Generation Prompt:**
```
I'm a [SERVICE TYPE] in [CITY/AREA]. Write a community-focused social media post about [A LOCAL BUSINESS, EVENT, OR COMMUNITY THING YOU WANT TO HIGHLIGHT].

What I love about them: [1-2 SENTENCES].

Make the post feel genuine — like a personal recommendation, not a paid ad. End with a question that invites engagement ("Who's your favorite...?" or "Have you tried...?").

Keep it under 80 words.
```

**Platform Notes:** Facebook (strong for local/community content), Instagram, Google Business.
**Time to Create:** 5 minutes
**Archetype Notes:**
- Overwhelmed Poster: Great for days when you have nothing else to post — it's easy and builds community goodwill
- Avoider: This can be done in person too — tell clients about other local businesses. Community connection doesn't require a post

### IDEA 6: The Retention Reminder

**Content Type:** Retention
**Purpose:** Gently reminds existing and past clients to rebook. Not pushy — helpful. Works because people genuinely forget.

**Template:**
> Quick reminder: if it's been [TIMEFRAME] since your last [SERVICE], your [RESULT] might be starting to [WHAT HAPPENS WHEN THEY WAIT TOO LONG].
>
> The good news? A quick [SERVICE/MAINTENANCE APPOINTMENT] can [POSITIVE OUTCOME].
>
> Don't wait until it's an emergency. Book your next one while you're thinking about it: [BOOKING LINK]

**AI Generation Prompt:**
```
I'm a [SERVICE TYPE]. Write a gentle rebooking reminder post for clients who might be overdue for [SERVICE].

Typical rebooking timeframe is every [TIMEFRAME — 4-6 weeks, monthly, quarterly, etc.].

What happens when they wait too long: [CONSEQUENCE — e.g., "color fading," "muscle tension building up," "skin issues getting harder to treat"].

Make it helpful, not guilt-trippy. Frame it as caring about their results, not nagging. End with a booking link.

Keep it under 80 words.
```

**Platform Notes:** Instagram Stories (with a poll: "When was your last [service]?"), Facebook, text message to past clients, email.
**Time to Create:** 5 minutes
**Archetype Notes:**
- Overwhelmed Poster: Schedule these monthly — they're easy and directly drive bookings
- Avoider: Send this as a personal text to 5 past clients instead of posting it. Same effect, no social media required

### IDEA 7: The Strategic Promotion

**Content Type:** Promotion
**Purpose:** Promotes your services without sounding desperate. Promotion should be strategic and occasional — max 1-2 per month mixed with other content types.

**Template:**
> This month, I'm [WHAT YOU'RE OFFERING — opening spots for X, running a special on Y, launching Z].
>
> [WHY — 1-2 sentences about why this matters or why now is a good time]
>
> [DETAILS — price, how to book, any limitations]
>
> [URGENCY — natural, not fake: "I have [X] spots available this month" or "This is available through [DATE]"]

**AI Generation Prompt:**
```
I'm a [SERVICE TYPE]. Write a promotional post for [WHAT YOU'RE PROMOTING — a specific service, seasonal offer, new availability, or special package].

Details:
- What it is: [DESCRIPTION]
- Price: [PRICE OR "regular pricing"]
- Availability: [SPOTS/TIMEFRAME]

Make it sound exciting but not desperate. Frame it as an opportunity, not a sale. Keep urgency real (limited spots, limited time) — no fake scarcity.

Keep it under 100 words. End with clear booking instructions.
```

**Platform Notes:** All platforms. Facebook and Instagram work well. Google Business posts are underused and effective for promotions.
**Time to Create:** 5-10 minutes
**Archetype Notes:**
- Overwhelmed Poster: Limit promotions to 1-2 per month. More than that trains people to wait for discounts
- Avoider: Send promotional offers directly to past clients via text or email — it's more personal and effective anyway

### IDEA 8: The FAQ Buster

**Content Type:** FAQ
**Purpose:** Answers questions people have but never ask. Removes objections before they become reasons not to book. Shows expertise.

**Template:**
> The #1 question I get: "[THE QUESTION — e.g., 'How long does X take?' or 'Is Y worth it?' or 'What's the difference between X and Y?']"
>
> Here's the honest answer: [YOUR ANSWER — 2-4 sentences, straightforward]
>
> [OPTIONAL: "Got a question? DM me — I'm happy to help."]

**AI Generation Prompt:**
```
I'm a [SERVICE TYPE]. Write a FAQ-style social media post answering this common question:

"[THE QUESTION YOUR CLIENTS FREQUENTLY ASK]"

My honest answer is: [YOUR ANSWER IN A FEW SENTENCES]

Make the post feel like I'm personally answering someone's question — not reading from a script. Keep it informative and reassuring. End with an invitation to ask more questions or book.

Keep it under 100 words.
```

**Platform Notes:** All platforms. Instagram carousels work well for "Top 5 Questions" series. Google Business posts are great for FAQ content (helps with search).
**Time to Create:** 5 minutes
**Archetype Notes:**
- Overwhelmed Poster: Turn your 5 most common questions into a content series — one per week for 5 weeks
- Avoider: Answer these questions in person or in your booking confirmation message. FAQ content doesn't have to be a social media post

### IDEA 9: The Engagement Driver

**Content Type:** Engagement
**Purpose:** Gets people interacting with your content, which tells the algorithm to show it to more people. Builds community. Starts conversations that can lead to bookings.

**Template:**
> Quick question for my [AUDIENCE TYPE — fellow beauty lovers / fitness people / local friends]:
>
> [THIS OR THAT QUESTION — "Do you prefer [option A] or [option B]?"]
>
> [OR: Poll format — "What's your biggest [TOPIC] struggle?" with 3-4 options]
>
> Drop your answer below — I'm curious!

**AI Generation Prompt:**
```
I'm a [SERVICE TYPE] in [CITY/AREA]. My ideal clients are [IDEAL CLIENT DESCRIPTION].

Give me 5 engagement post ideas — questions, polls, or "this or that" options that would get my ideal clients to respond and interact.

Make them fun and easy to answer (not serious business questions). They should relate to my industry but feel casual and lighthearted.

For each one, write the full post (under 50 words each).
```

**Platform Notes:** Instagram Stories (polls, question stickers), Facebook (questions in the feed), Instagram feed.
**Time to Create:** 3-5 minutes
**Archetype Notes:**
- Overwhelmed Poster: Use Instagram Stories polls — takes 30 seconds to create and drives engagement without writing a post
- Avoider: Ask these questions in person during appointments. The answers become content ideas for later (if you ever want to post)

### IDEA 10: The Credibility Marker

**Content Type:** Credibility
**Purpose:** Subtle proof that you're experienced and trustworthy. Not bragging — reflecting. Makes people feel confident booking with you.

**Template:**
> [NUMBER] years of [YOUR SERVICE TYPE] has taught me a lot. But here's the thing that surprises people most:
>
> [AN INSIGHT OR LESSON — something you've learned that clients might not expect]
>
> [PERSONAL REFLECTION — what this means for how you serve your clients]
>
> Experience isn't just about time. It's about [WHAT EXPERIENCE REALLY MEANS TO YOU].

**AI Generation Prompt:**
```
I'm a [SERVICE TYPE] with [YEARS] years of experience. Write a credibility-building post that:
- Mentions my experience naturally (not as bragging)
- Shares one unexpected lesson or insight I've gained
- Connects that lesson to how I serve my clients better
- Ends with a thought that makes people feel confident choosing me

Use this lesson/insight as the core: [YOUR LESSON — e.g., "the most important part of my job isn't the technical skill — it's listening" or "I've learned that less is usually more"]

Keep it under 100 words. Reflective and genuine tone.
```

**Platform Notes:** All platforms. Works especially well on Facebook (longer-form personal content performs well) and LinkedIn (if they're there).
**Time to Create:** 10 minutes
**Archetype Notes:**
- Overwhelmed Poster: Post this when you're feeling imposter syndrome — it reminds you (and your audience) of your value
- Avoider: Even if you never post this, write it for yourself. It's a grounding exercise in recognizing your own expertise
