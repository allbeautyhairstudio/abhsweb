## STAGE-SPECIFIC ROUTING NOTES

When reviewing the generated roadmap, adjust emphasis based on the client's stage:

### New / Building from Scratch
- **Emphasize:** Visibility, credibility, proof building
- **De-emphasize:** Retention (they don't have enough clients to retain yet)
- **Week 1 Card focus:** Google Business profile, first piece of content, ask for first review
- **Tone:** Encouraging, "you're building something real"

### Established but Inconsistent
- **Emphasize:** Systems, consistency, pipeline clarity
- **De-emphasize:** Major repositioning (what they have is working, it just needs structure)
- **Week 1 Card focus:** Set up weekly system, batch content day, re-engage 3 past clients
- **Tone:** Validating, "you have more than you think — it just needs a system"

### Fully Booked but Misaligned
- **Emphasize:** Better client attraction, pricing clarity, specialization
- **De-emphasize:** Volume/visibility (they don't need more clients, they need better ones)
- **Week 1 Card focus:** Identify ideal client messaging, review pricing, draft "who I serve" content
- **Tone:** Strategic, "let's upgrade your business, not just your bookings"

### Overwhelmed / Burned Out
- **Emphasize:** Simplification, retention over acquisition, fewer but better actions
- **De-emphasize:** Adding anything new. Focus on streamlining what exists
- **Week 1 Card focus:** ONE visibility action, decline one thing that drains them, reach out to 3 past clients
- **Tone:** Gentle, "less can be more — let's lighten the load"

### Stagnant / Plateaued
- **Emphasize:** Repositioning, new channels, offer restructuring
- **De-emphasize:** Doing more of the same
- **Week 1 Card focus:** Identify what's stale, try one new visibility channel, refresh their bio/about
- **Tone:** Energizing, "it's time for a reset — that's exactly what this is"

## ARCHETYPE ADJUSTMENTS

### For the Overwhelmed Poster
- Simplify the weekly system — fewer actions, not more
- In the 10 ideas section, star the 3 easiest ones
- Add a note: "You don't need to post every day. You need to post with purpose."
- Week 1 Card should include ONE content action, not three

### For the Avoider
- Lead with non-social-media options first
- Include text/email and in-person visibility actions
- Don't make social media feel mandatory
- Add a note: "Social media is ONE option — not the only one. Here are paths that don't require posting."
- Week 1 Card should focus on referrals, reviews, or direct outreach

---

## INTEREST FLAG HANDLING

### HIGH OPPORTUNITY flags
- Call these out in the roadmap's "What's Already Working" section
- Mention specific quick wins: "You're sitting on [X] — here's how to activate it"
- Prioritize in Week 1 Action Card

### WATCH flags
- Don't ignore them — address indirectly through system design
- If low confidence: add more affirmation language throughout
- If time-constrained: use the most simplified weekly system tier
- If burnout: lead with "what to stop doing" before "what to start"

### REDIRECT flags
- Include a gentle note in the roadmap: "One thing I want to flag..."
- Frame it constructively: "Before we focus on getting new clients, [the real bottleneck] may need some attention first"
- Still deliver the full roadmap — but note which pieces to prioritize after the underlying issue is addressed
- Be careful and compassionate. Never make them feel broken.
