## HOW TO USE THESE PROMPTS

1. Open your AI tool — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any AI chatbot
2. Find the prompt you need (organized by workflow)
3. Copy the entire prompt
4. Paste it into the AI tool
5. Replace everything in [BRACKETS] with your actual information
6. Hit enter
7. Review the output — if it doesn't feel right, see the "If It's Not Quite Right" section under each prompt

**Important:** These prompts work in ANY AI tool. You don't need a specific one. Use whatever you're most comfortable with.

## WORKFLOW 1: THE CONTENT ENGINE — Prompts

---

### CE-1: Content Idea Brainstorm

**When to use:** Start of your content creation session. Gets you 7-10 ideas to choose from.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
I'm a [YOUR SERVICE TYPE] based in [YOUR CITY/AREA]. My ideal clients are [DESCRIBE YOUR IDEAL CLIENT IN 1-2 SENTENCES]. My most popular services are [YOUR TOP 2-3 SERVICES].

Give me 10 content ideas I can post on [YOUR PLATFORM — Instagram/Facebook/etc.] this week. Mix these types:
- 2 educational (teach something useful)
- 2 trust-building (show why someone should choose me)
- 2 social proof (highlight results or client experiences)
- 2 behind-the-scenes (show my personality or process)
- 2 engagement (get people talking or responding)

Keep each idea to 1-2 sentences. Make them specific to my business, not generic.
```

**What you'll get:** A list of 10 specific content ideas tailored to your business.

**If it's not quite right:**
- Too generic? Add: "Make these more specific to [your specialty]. I'm known for [what makes you different]."
- Too complicated? Add: "Keep these simple — each post should take me under 10 minutes to create."
- Want seasonal ideas? Add: "It's [current month/season]. Include ideas relevant to this time of year."

---

### CE-2: Post Drafter

**When to use:** After you've picked an idea from your brainstorm. Turns one idea into a ready-to-post caption.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
Write a [PLATFORM — Instagram/Facebook/etc.] post for my [YOUR SERVICE TYPE] business.

The topic is: [PASTE THE CONTENT IDEA YOU PICKED]

My business name is [YOUR BUSINESS NAME] and I'm based in [YOUR CITY/AREA].

Write it in a [PICK YOUR TONE: warm and friendly / professional but approachable / casual and fun / confident and direct] voice.

Keep it under 150 words. Include a call to action at the end (like asking a question, inviting them to book, or encouraging them to share). Don't use hashtags yet — I'll add those separately.
```

**What you'll get:** A ready-to-edit post with a call to action.

**If it's not quite right:**
- Too formal? Say: "Rewrite this like I'm talking to a friend who just asked me about this."
- Too long? Say: "Make this shorter — under 75 words."
- Doesn't sound like you? Say: "I tend to be more [your style]. Adjust the tone."

---

### CE-3: Post Revision

**When to use:** When the drafted post doesn't feel right and you want a different version.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
Here's a post I drafted but it doesn't feel right:

[PASTE THE POST YOU WANT TO REVISE]

What I don't like about it: [EXPLAIN WHAT FEELS OFF — too salesy, too long, doesn't sound like me, too generic, etc.]

Rewrite it keeping the same main idea but make it [WHAT YOU WANT INSTEAD — shorter, more personal, more casual, more educational, less pushy, etc.].
```

**What you'll get:** A revised version that addresses your specific concern.

---

### CE-4: Hashtag & Caption Enhancer

**When to use:** After your post is drafted and you want to add hashtags and optimize the caption.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
Here's my [PLATFORM] post:

[PASTE YOUR FINAL POST]

My business is a [YOUR SERVICE TYPE] in [YOUR CITY/AREA].

Give me:
1. 15-20 relevant hashtags (mix of popular, niche, and local)
2. A stronger opening line if mine could be better (the first line people see before "...more")
3. Any emoji suggestions that fit naturally (don't overdo it)
```

**What you'll get:** Hashtags, an optimized hook, and optional emoji placement.

---

### CE-5: Content Repurposer

**When to use:** When you have a post that performed well and want to adapt it for another platform or format.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
Here's a post that did well on [ORIGINAL PLATFORM]:

[PASTE YOUR SUCCESSFUL POST]

Repurpose this for [NEW PLATFORM OR FORMAT — Instagram Story / Facebook post / Google Business update / email to clients / text message to past clients].

Keep the core message but adjust the format and length for [NEW PLATFORM]. Add a call to action appropriate for that platform.
```

**What you'll get:** Your successful content adapted for a new platform.

## WORKFLOW 2: THE CLIENT MAGNET — Prompts

---

### CM-1: Engagement Response

**When to use:** Someone commented on your post, asked a question in your DMs, or showed interest in your services.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
I'm a [YOUR SERVICE TYPE]. Someone just [DESCRIBE WHAT THEY DID — commented on my post asking about X / DM'd me saying Y / asked me in person about Z].

Help me write a response that:
1. Acknowledges what they said (shows I'm listening)
2. Gives them a helpful answer or tip
3. Gently opens the door to booking without being pushy

Keep it conversational — like I'm texting a friend, not writing a sales pitch. Under 75 words.
```

**What you'll get:** A natural, helpful response that moves them toward booking.

**If it's not quite right:**
- Too salesy? Say: "Make this softer. I don't want to push — just help."
- Too casual? Say: "Make this a bit more professional while still being warm."

---

### CM-2: Follow-Up Message

**When to use:** Someone showed interest but didn't book. You want to follow up without being pushy.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
I had a conversation with a potential client about [WHAT YOU DISCUSSED]. They seemed interested but didn't book.

Write a follow-up message I can send [HOW — text / DM / email] that:
1. References our previous conversation naturally
2. Adds one more piece of value (a tip, helpful info, or answer to a common question about [YOUR SERVICE])
3. Makes booking easy by including my booking link
4. Doesn't feel pushy or desperate

Keep it under 60 words. Warm and genuine.
```

**What you'll get:** A natural follow-up that doesn't feel like a sales chase.

---

### CM-3: New Client Welcome

**When to use:** Someone just booked. Send this to set expectations and build excitement.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
A new client just booked a [SERVICE TYPE] appointment with me. Their name is [CLIENT NAME].

Write a short welcome message I can send via [text / DM / email] that:
1. Thanks them for booking
2. Builds excitement about their upcoming appointment
3. Tells them anything they should know beforehand (like [ANY PREP INSTRUCTIONS — arrive 10 min early, come with clean hair, etc.])
4. Makes them feel like they made a great decision

Keep it warm and under 80 words.
```

**What you'll get:** A welcome message that turns a booking into a positive experience before they even walk in.

---

### CM-4: Pricing Response

**When to use:** Someone asks "How much do you charge?" — the #1 question that freezes most service providers.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
I'm a [YOUR SERVICE TYPE]. Someone just asked me how much I charge for [SPECIFIC SERVICE].

My price for this is [YOUR PRICE].

Write a response that:
1. Shares the price confidently (no apologizing)
2. Briefly explains what's included or what makes it worth it
3. Invites them to book or ask more questions
4. Feels warm, not transactional

Keep it under 60 words. I want to send this via [text / DM].
```

**What you'll get:** A confident, value-framed pricing response.

**If it's not quite right:**
- Want to offer a range? Say: "I actually have different levels: [list them with prices]. Help me present these as options."

---

### CM-5: Handling Hesitation

**When to use:** A potential client expresses doubt, pushback, or hesitation (too expensive, not sure they need it, etc.).

**Copy and paste this:**

```
A potential client said: "[WHAT THEY SAID — e.g., 'That's a little more than I expected' or 'I need to think about it' or 'I've been doing it myself']"

They were asking about [YOUR SERVICE].

Write a response that:
1. Validates their concern (don't dismiss it)
2. Gently reframes the value
3. Leaves the door open without pressure
4. Stays warm and genuine — not defensive

Keep it under 75 words.
```

**What you'll get:** A calm, confident response to common objections.

## WORKFLOW 3: THE RETENTION LOOP — Prompts

---

### RL-1: Client Check-In

**When to use:** Weekly. Pick 3-5 recent clients and send a quick personal message.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
I'm a [YOUR SERVICE TYPE]. I saw a client named [CLIENT NAME] recently for [SERVICE THEY GOT].

Write a short check-in message I can text or DM them that:
1. References something specific about their visit (like "[SOMETHING SPECIFIC — their new color, the style we tried, the issue we worked on]")
2. Gives them one useful aftercare or maintenance tip
3. Makes rebooking easy by mentioning my availability or including my booking link

Keep it personal, warm, and under 50 words. It should feel like a text from someone who cares, not a marketing message.
```

**What you'll get:** A personal check-in that keeps you top of mind and encourages rebooking.

---

### RL-2: Review Request

**When to use:** Monthly. Send to 3-5 clients who had great experiences.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
I'm a [YOUR SERVICE TYPE] and I'd like to ask a happy client for a Google review (or [YELP / FACEBOOK / OTHER PLATFORM]).

Their name is [CLIENT NAME] and they recently got [SERVICE]. They [WHAT MADE THEIR EXPERIENCE GREAT — loved their results, said they'd definitely come back, etc.].

Write a short, personal message asking them to leave a review. Include:
1. A genuine thank-you
2. Why their review matters to my business (keep it honest, not dramatic)
3. A direct link to my review page: [YOUR GOOGLE REVIEW LINK or "I'll add my link"]

Keep it under 60 words. Make it feel like a personal ask, not a mass message.
```

**What you'll get:** A sincere review request that gets responses.

**If it's not quite right:**
- Too formal? Say: "Make this more casual — like a friendly text."
- Want to incentivize? Add: "Also mention that I'm offering [incentive — $10 off next visit, a free add-on, etc.] as a thank-you for leaving a review."

---

### RL-3: Referral Ask

**When to use:** Monthly. Send to your happiest, most connected clients.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
I'm a [YOUR SERVICE TYPE]. I want to ask one of my favorite clients, [CLIENT NAME], to refer friends or family.

My referral incentive is: [YOUR INCENTIVE — $50 off their next visit / a free add-on / 15% off their next service / etc.]

Write a personal message that:
1. Tells them I appreciate them as a client
2. Mentions the referral incentive naturally (not as a sales pitch)
3. Makes it easy — "Just send them my way and mention your name"
4. Feels like a conversation, not a business request

Keep it under 70 words.
```

**What you'll get:** A referral ask that feels natural and appreciative.

---

### RL-4: Post-Appointment Follow-Up

**When to use:** After a great appointment. Send within 24 hours while the experience is fresh.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
I just finished an appointment with [CLIENT NAME]. They got [SERVICE] and [HOW IT WENT — they loved it, the results were great, we tried something new, etc.].

Write a quick follow-up message I can text or DM that:
1. Says something warm about seeing them today
2. Includes one specific aftercare or maintenance tip for [THEIR SERVICE]
3. Mentions when they might want to come back ("Usually I recommend rebooking in [TIMEFRAME] for [SERVICE]")
4. Includes my booking link

Keep it under 50 words. Genuine, not robotic.
```

**What you'll get:** A quick follow-up that increases rebooking rates.

---

### RL-5: Lapsed Client Re-Engagement

**When to use:** When a regular client hasn't been in for a while and you want to reconnect.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
I'm a [YOUR SERVICE TYPE]. A regular client named [CLIENT NAME] usually comes in every [THEIR USUAL FREQUENCY — 4-6 weeks, monthly, etc.] for [THEIR USUAL SERVICE], but I haven't seen them in [HOW LONG — 2 months, 3 months, etc.].

Write a warm, no-pressure message to reconnect. Include:
1. A genuine "hope you're doing well"
2. Something new or seasonal I could mention: [ANYTHING NEW — new service, seasonal offer, updated hours, or just "nothing specific"]
3. An easy way to rebook: [YOUR BOOKING LINK]

Make it feel like a friend checking in — not a "we miss you!" marketing email. Under 50 words.
```

**What you'll get:** A natural reconnection that brings lapsed clients back.

## BONUS PROMPTS

---

### BP-1: Bio/About Me Writer

**When to use:** When you need to update your social media bio, Google Business description, or website about section.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
I'm a [YOUR SERVICE TYPE] based in [YOUR CITY/AREA]. I specialize in [YOUR SPECIALTIES]. I've been doing this for [YEARS IN BUSINESS] years.

My ideal clients are [WHO YOU LOVE WORKING WITH].

What makes me different is [WHAT SETS YOU APART — your approach, your training, your personality, your specialty].

Write a [PLATFORM — Instagram bio (under 150 characters) / Google Business description (under 750 characters) / website about section (2-3 paragraphs)] that:
1. Makes it clear what I do and who I serve
2. Shows my personality
3. Includes a call to action (book now, DM me, visit my website)
4. Doesn't use cheesy marketing language
```

**What you'll get:** A polished bio that attracts the right clients.

---

### BP-2: Service Description Writer

**When to use:** When you need to write or improve descriptions for your services (for your website, booking page, or social media).

**Copy and paste this:**

```
I'm a [YOUR SERVICE TYPE]. Write a description for this service:

Service name: [SERVICE NAME]
What it is: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF WHAT YOU DO]
How long it takes: [DURATION]
Price: [PRICE OR PRICE RANGE]
Who it's best for: [TYPE OF CLIENT]
What makes mine different: [YOUR UNIQUE APPROACH]

Write it in [FIRST PERSON — "I" / THIRD PERSON — "she/he/they"] voice. Keep it under 100 words. Make it sound inviting, clear, and professional — not salesy.
```

**What you'll get:** A service description that sells without trying too hard.

---

### BP-3: Monthly Content Calendar

**When to use:** At the start of each month to plan your content for the next 4 weeks.

**Copy and paste this:**

```
I'm a [YOUR SERVICE TYPE] based in [YOUR CITY/AREA]. I post on [YOUR PLATFORMS] about [HOW OFTEN — once a week, 3x/week, etc.].

Create a content calendar for [MONTH/YEAR] with [NUMBER OF POSTS] posts.

For each post, give me:
- Suggested date
- Content type (educational, social proof, behind-the-scenes, engagement, etc.)
- Post idea (1-2 sentences)
- Suggested visual (photo type or Canva template idea)

Mix up the content types. Include anything seasonal or timely for [MONTH]. Keep ideas specific to my industry.
```

**What you'll get:** A month of content planned in one sitting.

## PROMPT TIPS FOR ALL WORKFLOWS

**Getting better results from any prompt:**
1. **Be specific.** "I'm a hairstylist who specializes in balayage for brunettes" gets better results than "I'm a hairstylist"
2. **Provide examples.** "Write it like this example I liked: [example]" helps the AI match your style
3. **Iterate.** The first output is a draft. Say "Make it shorter" or "More casual" or "Try a different angle" to refine it
4. **Use your own words.** Always review and edit AI output before posting. Add your voice, your details, your personality

**If you're brand new to AI:**
- Start with just ONE prompt. Try CE-1 (brainstorm) and see what happens
- You can't break anything. If the output is bad, just try again
- Think of it as a brainstorming partner, not a finished product machine
- Copy the prompt exactly as written. Then replace the [BRACKETS]. That's it
