## HOW TO USE THESE WORKFLOWS

You have 3 workflows. You don't need to use all 3 right away.

**Start with the one that matches your biggest need:**
- Need content but don't know what to post? → **Workflow 1: The Content Engine**
- Getting engagement but not bookings? → **Workflow 2: The Client Magnet**
- Have clients but they don't come back or refer? → **Workflow 3: The Retention Loop**

Each workflow has:
- **When to use it** (the trigger)
- **Step-by-step instructions** (numbered, plain language)
- **Which prompts to use** (from your prompt sheet — `ncworkflowprompts.md`)
- **How long it takes**
- **What you'll end up with**

No AI experience required. If you can copy, paste, and type — you can do this.

## WORKFLOW 1: THE CONTENT ENGINE

### What It Does
Helps you create a batch of 5-7 posts in one sitting so you're not scrambling for ideas every day.

### When to Use It
**Trigger:** It's your content creation day (pick one day per week or every other week).

### Who This Is Best For
- **Overwhelmed Posters:** You're already posting — this makes it strategic instead of random
- **Avoiders:** Do this once every 2 weeks. Batch it. Get it done. Move on with your life

### Time Investment
- First time: 45-60 minutes (you're learning the process)
- After that: 25-40 minutes per batch

### Step-by-Step Instructions

**Step 1: Brainstorm (5 minutes)**
Open your AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — whatever you have). Copy and paste **Prompt CE-1** (Brainstorm Prompt) from your prompt sheet. Fill in the [PLACEHOLDERS] with your business details. Hit enter.

You'll get 7-10 content ideas. Pick 5-7 that feel right for you this week.

**Step 2: Draft (15-20 minutes)**
For each idea you picked, copy and paste **Prompt CE-2** (Draft Prompt) from your prompt sheet. Fill in the [PLACEHOLDERS]. Hit enter.

You'll get a ready-to-edit post for each idea. Read through them. Adjust anything that doesn't sound like you.

**Step 3: Customize (5-10 minutes)**
Make each post yours. Add:
- Your own words or phrases
- A specific detail about your business or a recent client (with their permission)
- Your personality — sarcasm, humor, warmth, whatever is authentically you

If a post doesn't feel right, use **Prompt CE-3** (Revision Prompt) to ask for a different version.

**Step 4: Add Visuals (5-10 minutes)**
For each post, decide on a visual:
- Photo from your camera roll (work photos, behind-the-scenes, your space)
- Simple graphic (Canva has free templates)
- Text-only post (these work great on Facebook and as Instagram stories)

**Step 5: Schedule or Save (5 minutes)**
Either:
- Schedule posts using your platform's built-in scheduler (Instagram, Facebook, Later, etc.)
- Save them in your Notes app and post one per day or every few days
- Copy them into a Google Doc labeled "Content Bank — [Month]"

### What You End Up With
5-7 posts ready to go — no daily scrambling, no staring at a blank screen.

### Tips
- Don't aim for perfect. Aim for done.
- If you only have 20 minutes, do Steps 1-2 and skip customization. AI-drafted content you actually post beats perfect content you never publish.
- Keep a running "idea list" on your phone. When something happens at work that's interesting, jot it down. Use these as inputs for Step 1 next time.

## WORKFLOW 2: THE CLIENT MAGNET

### What It Does
Helps you turn engagement (likes, comments, DMs, questions) into actual bookings. Most service providers get some attention but don't know how to move people from "interested" to "booked."

### When to Use It
**Trigger:** Any of these happen:
- Someone comments on your post or story
- Someone DMs you a question
- Someone asks "How much do you charge?"
- A friend or acquaintance mentions they need your service
- You meet someone at an event who could be a client

### Who This Is Best For
- **Overwhelmed Posters:** You're getting engagement — let's turn it into bookings
- **Avoiders:** Even without social media, people ask you questions in person. This workflow helps you convert those conversations too

### Time Investment
- Per interaction: 3-5 minutes
- Weekly (processing all engagement): 15-20 minutes

### Step-by-Step Instructions

**Step 1: Identify the Opportunity (1 minute)**
When someone engages — DM, comment, question, or in-person mention — ask yourself:
> "Is this person a potential client, or just being social?"

If they're asking about your services, pricing, availability, or mentioning a problem you solve — they're a potential client. Move to Step 2.

**Step 2: Respond with Value (2-3 minutes)**
Don't just answer their question — add something useful. Use **Prompt CM-1** (Engagement Response Prompt) to draft a response if you're not sure what to say.

**The formula:**
1. Acknowledge what they said (show you're listening)
2. Answer their question or give a helpful tip
3. Open the door: "If you ever want to [service], I'd love to help — here's how to book: [link]"

**Step 3: Follow Up (2 minutes — next day)**
If they seemed interested but didn't book, follow up once. Not pushy — helpful.

Use **Prompt CM-2** (Follow-Up Prompt) to draft a natural follow-up message.

**The formula:**
1. Reference your previous conversation
2. Add one more piece of value (a tip, an answer to a common question)
3. Make booking easy: "No pressure — just wanted to make sure you had my booking link if you decide to: [link]"

**Step 4: If They Book — Welcome Them (2 minutes)**
Send a quick welcome message. Use **Prompt CM-3** (New Client Welcome Prompt).

This sets expectations, builds excitement, and makes them feel like they made a great decision.

**Step 5: If They Don't Book — No Worries (0 minutes)**
Not everyone will book. That's normal. You planted a seed. They know who you are now.

Don't follow up more than once. Don't take it personally. Move on.

### What You End Up With
A consistent way to turn interest into bookings — without being salesy or pushy.

### Tips
- Speed matters. Respond to DMs and questions within a few hours, not a few days.
- You don't need to use AI for every interaction — but when you're stuck on what to say, the prompts help.
- Keep your booking link in your phone's keyboard shortcuts or notes. Make it one tap to share.
- Track your conversions loosely: "This week, 3 people asked. 1 booked." Over time, you'll see patterns.

## WORKFLOW 3: THE RETENTION LOOP

### What It Does
Keeps existing clients coming back AND activates referrals — the two most cost-effective ways to grow a service business.

### When to Use It
**Triggers:**
- End of each week: Quick check-in batch
- End of each month: Referral + review push
- After a great appointment: Immediate follow-up
- When you notice a regular client hasn't been in for a while: Re-engagement

### Who This Is Best For
- **Overwhelmed Posters:** Your existing clients are your best marketing channel — this activates them
- **Avoiders:** This workflow barely requires social media. Most of it is text, email, or in-person conversation

### Time Investment
- Weekly check-ins: 10-15 minutes
- Monthly referral push: 15-20 minutes
- Per follow-up after appointment: 2-3 minutes

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### WEEKLY: Client Check-In Batch (10-15 minutes)

**Step 1: Pull Up Your Recent Clients (2 minutes)**
Look at who you saw this week. Pick 3-5 clients who had a great experience or who you haven't heard from in a while.

**Step 2: Send a Quick Check-In (5-10 minutes)**
Use **Prompt RL-1** (Client Check-In Prompt) to draft a quick, personal message for each client.

**The formula:**
1. Reference something specific about their visit ("How's the new color holding up?")
2. Add a useful tip related to their service
3. Make rebooking easy: "When you're ready for your next [service], here's my link: [booking link]"

Send via text, DM, or email — whatever you normally use to communicate with clients.

**Step 3: Log It (1 minute)**
Keep a simple tracker (Notes app, spreadsheet, whatever works):
- Who you reached out to
- When
- Did they respond? Rebook?

This takes 60 seconds and helps you see patterns over time.

#### MONTHLY: Referral & Review Push (15-20 minutes)

**Step 1: Identify Your Happy Clients (3 minutes)**
Think about your top 5-10 clients from the past month — the ones who raved about their service, left happy, or told you they'd come back.

**Step 2: Ask for Reviews (5-7 minutes)**
Use **Prompt RL-2** (Review Request Prompt) to draft a personal review request.

**The formula:**
1. Thank them for being a client
2. Tell them their review genuinely helps your business
3. Make it dead simple: include the direct link to your Google review page (or Yelp, or wherever)

Send to 3-5 clients per month. You don't need 100 reviews — you need 10-20 good ones.

**Step 3: Activate Referrals (5-7 minutes)**
Use **Prompt RL-3** (Referral Ask Prompt) to draft a referral message.

**The referral incentive:**
> "Know someone who could use [your service]? Send them my way and I'll take $[amount or offer] off your next appointment as a thank-you."

Send to 3-5 clients who you know are happy and connected. Personal text or DM works best.

**Step 4: Follow Up on Previous Referral Asks (2-3 minutes)**
Check if anyone you asked last month actually referred someone. If they did — thank them and deliver the incentive.

#### AS NEEDED: Post-Appointment Follow-Up (2-3 minutes)

After a particularly great appointment:

Use **Prompt RL-4** (Post-Appointment Follow-Up Prompt) to send a quick message.

**The formula:**
1. "It was so great seeing you today!"
2. One specific aftercare tip related to their service
3. "Can't wait for your next visit — your link is always here: [booking link]"

This takes 2 minutes and dramatically increases rebooking rates.

#### AS NEEDED: Re-Engagement (for Lapsed Clients)

When you notice a regular hasn't been in for a while:

Use **Prompt RL-5** (Re-Engagement Prompt) to reach out.

**The formula:**
1. "Hey [name] — it's been a while! Hope everything's going well."
2. Add value: share something new you're offering, or a seasonal tip
3. Open the door: "If you're ready to come in, I have some availability [timeframe] — [booking link]"

No guilt. No pressure. Just a warm reconnect.

### What You End Up With
- Existing clients rebooking more consistently
- A steady stream of Google/Yelp reviews building over time
- Referrals coming in without paid advertising
- A reputation as someone who genuinely cares about their clients

### Tips
- Texts get higher response rates than emails or DMs. If you have a client's phone number, text them.
- Don't wait for the "perfect" moment to ask for a referral. If someone says "I love my hair!" — that IS the moment.
- Batch your weekly check-ins on the same day each week. Make it a habit.
- The referral incentive doesn't have to be huge. $25-$50 off is enough. The ask is what matters, not the reward.

## FOR KARLI: SESSION DELIVERY NOTES

When presenting workflows in the session:
1. **Don't present all 3 equally.** Based on the Master Analysis, recommend which 1-2 are highest priority for this specific client
2. **Demo one prompt live.** Open ChatGPT during the session, paste in a prompt using their business details, and show them the output. This builds confidence
3. **Acknowledge overwhelm proactively:** "I know this looks like a lot written down — but you're only doing ONE of these to start. Pick the one that resonates most."
4. **For Avoiders:** Lead with Workflow 3 (Retention Loop) — it barely requires social media and leverages their existing client relationships
5. **For Overwhelmed Posters:** Lead with Workflow 1 (Content Engine) — it replaces their random posting with strategic batching
