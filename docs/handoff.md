## 2. BUSINESS CONCEPT OVERVIEW <a name="business-concept-overview"></a>

**Service Name:** AI Marketing Reset for Service-Based Businesses

**One-Sentence Positioning:**
A one-time marketing reset that gives service providers a clear, simple system to attract clients consistently — without spending hours on social media or hiring an expensive agency.

**What It Is:**
- A bounded, personalized, one-time diagnostic + system build
- Delivered via a 90-minute strategy session
- Powered by AI analysis behind the scenes (client doesn't need to understand AI)
- Focused on clarity, simplicity, and capacity-aware execution

**What It Is NOT:**
- Ongoing marketing management
- Social media management
- A course or information dump
- A guarantee of specific results
- An agency retainer

**Core Deliverables:**
1. Visibility & client flow assessment (where clients come from, where they're lost)
2. Marketing maturity diagnostic (stage, readiness, emotional state)
3. Attention leak analysis (top 5 places they're losing potential clients)
4. Simple, capacity-aware client-attraction system (weekly plan)
5. 3 AI-powered content workflows (with copy-paste prompts)
6. 10 ready-to-post/use visibility ideas tailored to their business
7. 1 high-impact referral or retention opportunity
8. 90-day focus plan (Month 1 / Month 2 / Month 3)
9. Week 1 Action Card (3 immediate, dead-simple first steps)

**The Promise:**
"Walk away with a 30-day AI-powered client attraction system you can execute in under 2 hours per week."

## 7. INTAKE FORM (FINAL DRAFT) <a name="intake-form"></a>

**Platform:** JotForm (familiar to Karli, exports PDF, supports conditional logic)
**Estimated completion time:** 10-12 minutes
**Conditional logic:** Section 7 branches based on social media activity (Yes vs No/Rarely)

### INTRODUCTION (Client-Facing)

> **Before we begin...**
>
> This intake helps me understand your business, your capacity, and the kind of clients you want more of.
>
> Some questions may feel new. Some you may not have thought about before. That's completely okay.
>
> The goal isn't perfect answers. The goal is direction.
>
> Together, this helps us clarify:
> - how you want your business to be perceived
> - the type of clients you want to attract
> - what is (and isn't) working right now
> - how to attract the right clients without overwhelm
>
> Building and maintaining a steady client base at any stage of business can feel daunting. There is no magic wand. What we are creating is clarity — and a simple system designed around your real capacity and lifestyle.
>
> Answer honestly. Simple answers are perfect.

### EXPECTATIONS & SCOPE

> **What this reset provides:**
> - clarity on where your clients are coming from (and where they're not)
> - a simple, sustainable client-attraction system
> - guidance tailored to your capacity and goals
> - practical next steps you can implement immediately
>
> **What this reset is not:**
> - ongoing marketing management
> - social media management
> - a guarantee of specific results
> - a requirement to spend hours online
>
> Your results will come from consistent implementation of the simple system we build together.
>
> **A note about modern marketing:**
> Most service providers were trained to perfect their craft — not to navigate modern marketing. If this feels confusing or overwhelming, you're not alone. There are no wrong answers here. This process simply helps tailor your reset to where you are right now.

1. Business name

18. Which best describes your current marketing approach? (Don't market at all / Post occasionally / Post regularly no results / Rely on referrals / Tried things nothing clear / Overwhelmed by modern marketing)

**IF YES:**

**IF NO or RARELY:**
25. What has stopped you from being active? (time / anxiety or discomfort / not sure what to post / doesn't seem to work / not interested / other)

### SECTION 8: Offers & Pricing

### INTAKE FORM — IDENTIFIED GAPS (from Claude assessment)

These were flagged during review and should be addressed before beta:

1. **Overlap between Sections 2, 3, and 6** — "current goal," "current stage," "current marketing approach," and "what feels hardest" circle similar territory. Consider consolidating Section 3 into Section 2.

2. **Section 4 question wording** — "Describe your ideal client" is too open-ended for people who've never thought about it. Suggested rewrite: "Think about your favorite client — the one you wish you had 10 more of. What makes them great to work with?"

3. **Missing: Average service price and highest-priced service** — Needed to determine if the strategy should focus on volume vs. premium positioning. Add to Section 8.

4. **Missing: Monthly new client volume** — "How many new clients do you typically get per month?" Determines whether the problem is traffic or conversion.

5. **Online presence section should not be optional** — Website, social links, and booking links are essential for AI analysis. Make required with "N/A" options.

6. **Missing: Trust/proof assets question** — "Do you have testimonials? Google reviews? Before/after photos?" was in earlier drafts but got cut. This is a conversion lever — add it back.

7. **Missing: Consent/agreement checkbox** — "I understand this reset provides clarity and a system — results depend on my implementation." Protects scope and expectations.

## 9. POSITIONING & MESSAGING GUIDELINES <a name="positioning-and-messaging"></a>

### Use This Language:
- "clarity" over "strategy"
- "simple system" over "marketing plan"
- "sustainable" over "scalable"
- "designed around your capacity" over "growth hacking"
- "stop guessing" over "optimize your funnel"
- "attract the right clients" over "generate leads"
- "reset" (implies recalibration, not failure)

### Avoid This Language:
- Hustle culture ("10x your bookings!")
- Corporate jargon (ROI, KPIs, conversion rates, funnels)
- Specific result promises ("fill your books in 30 days")
- Anything implying they're "doing it wrong" — frame as "what's next" not "what's broken"
- Don't lead with "AI" as the selling point — AI is the ingredient, not the headline

### Key Differentiators:
- Bounded deliverable (not ongoing dependency)
- Affordable (not agency pricing)
- Personalized (not a course)
- Capacity-aware (respects their body, time, and energy)
- Built by someone who understands their world (not a marketing bro)

### The White Space:
Nobody is specifically combining AI literacy + marketing strategy + capacity awareness in a single, bounded deliverable for solo service providers. That is the differentiation.

ROADMAP GENERATION

        |                 - Business summary + positive callouts

        |                 - Interest flags (opportunity/watch/redirect)

        |

  ncworkflow.md ─────── 3 step-by-step workflows they follow

        |

### Design Principles Applied Across All Files

- **Tone:** Per Section 9 — "clarity" not "strategy," "simple system" not "marketing plan," "what's next" not "what's broken"
- **Scalability:** 80% templated framework, 20% personalization per client
- **Time cap:** All backend work must fit within 2.5-3 hours per client
- **Accessibility:** All client-facing materials must be usable by someone with zero AI experience
- **Stage-aware:** All prompts route differently based on the 5 client stages
- **Archetype-aware:** All workflows address both the Overwhelmed Poster and the Avoider
- **Edge cases:** All analysis prompts catch burnout, non-viable businesses, emotional redirects
- **AI-agnostic:** Client prompts work in ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any AI tool
- **Capacity-aware:** Weekly systems calibrated to 4 time tiers (under 30min / 30-60min / 1-2hrs / 2+hrs)
